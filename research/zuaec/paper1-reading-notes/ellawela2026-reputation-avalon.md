# Trust, Lies, and Long Memories: Emergent Social Dynamics and Reputation in Multi-Round Avalon with LLM Agents
**arXiv**: 2604.20582 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: Ellawela | **Venue**: arXiv preprint

## Core Claim
LLM agents with cross-game memory in repeated Resistance: Avalon develop stable role-conditional reputations and sophisticated deception strategies, demonstrating emergent social reasoning including "sleeper agent" trust-building.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- "Subtle" descriptor applied to one agent 38 times across games; 105+ instances of explicit past-behavior referencing
- Role-conditional: "straightforward" 27× when good, 0× when evil (same agent)
- High-reputation players received 45.6% more team inclusions (4.84 vs. 3.32 games)
- Sleeper agent strategy: evil players passed early missions in 75% of high-reasoning games vs. 36% low-reasoning games
- 188 games across 5 LLM agents; 50-game reputation tournament
- GPT-5.1 with ReAct + memory system; 3 reasoning levels (Low/Medium/High)

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: §4 social dynamics — empirical evidence of emergent reputation formation and strategic role performance in LLM agents
- **§4 use**: Supports S (Social Role Agency) pillar — agents adapt behavior to assigned roles over time (good vs. evil role conditioning); demonstrates that role identity shapes behavioral trajectory, not just single-turn responses
- Cross-game memory enabling reputation is analogous to persistent identity in ASM — motivates P pillar infrastructure
