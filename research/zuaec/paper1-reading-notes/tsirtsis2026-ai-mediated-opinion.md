# AI-Mediated Communication Can Steer Collective Opinion
**arXiv**: 2605.16245 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: Tsirtsis et al. | **Venue**: arXiv preprint (cs.CY)

## Core Claim
When LLMs mediate human-to-human communication on social platforms, their directional biases propagate through social networks and systematically shift collective opinion formation — demonstrated empirically with X/Grok and theoretically via opinion dynamics modeling.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Empirical: LLMs from multiple families introduced directional biases when editing texts on contested topics (gun control, atheism)
- Grok "Explain this post" feature showed pro-life bias on abortion content, traceable to design choices
- Mathematical model with real social network data demonstrates network amplification of LLM bias
- Equilibrium state analysis characterizing long-run opinion distribution under AI mediation

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: §5 societal risks — LLM-mediated communication as a systemic risk vector for opinion steering
- **§5 use**: Uniquely focuses on AI as an intermediary (not a direct participant) still shaping collective belief; supports argument that even "neutral" communication infrastructure can carry values/biases — motivates protocol-level transparency requirements
- Distinct from Zhong2025 (emergent SoS) and He2026 (intentional manipulation): this is unintentional structural bias

## Paper 2 Relevance
§5 risk + §2 motivation — if communication protocols embed biases, role-bearing agents with transparent principals are more auditable; ASM's explicit role/principal binding addresses accountability gap.

## Verdict
⭐⭐⭐ | Include (§5 risks) — real-world case (Grok) + theoretical model; strong credibility for risk framing
