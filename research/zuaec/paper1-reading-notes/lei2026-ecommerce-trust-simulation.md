# Strategic Exploitation in LLM Agent Markets: A Simulation Framework for E-Commerce Trust
**arXiv**: 2605.10059 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: Lei et al. | **Venue**: arXiv preprint (cs.AI)

## Core Claim
LLM agents autonomously discover and exploit weaknesses in reputation-based e-commerce governance systems; warrant enforcement mechanisms can mitigate deceptive behavior and reshape strategic reasoning under information asymmetry.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- TruthMarketTwin simulation framework for bilateral trade with information asymmetry (sellers know quality, buyers don't)
- LLM agents autonomously exploit reputation-based governance weaknesses
- Warrant enforcement reduces deception and alters strategic decision-making
- Claims to be "one of the first agent-based models examining e-commerce under asymmetric information"
- Full quantitative metrics not available in abstract

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: §4 social dynamics / §5 risks — trust exploitation in agent markets as evidence that N (Norm/Governance) pillar requires active enforcement mechanisms
- **§4 use**: Supports argument that reputation alone is insufficient for agent-market governance; connects to institutional design requirements (Fei2026, Bracale2026)
- Lower specificity than DEBATE or Zhong2025 — limited numbers in abstract

## Paper 2 Relevance
§5 risk analysis — autonomous exploitation of governance gaps is a direct threat model for ASM open networks; motivates warrant/attestation mechanisms in protocol design.
