# LLM Agents Make Collective Belief Dynamics Programmable: Challenges and Research Directions
**arXiv**: 2605.19915 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: He et al. | **Venue**: arXiv preprint (cs.MA)

## Core Claim
LLM-based agents represent a fundamental shift enabling coordinated, large-scale manipulation of collective beliefs — "programmable collective belief control" — with four structural properties (indistinguishability, persistence, contextuality, configurability) making this uniquely dangerous compared to prior influence operations.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Proof-of-concept simulations show coordinated AI agents can induce measurable belief shifts stabilizing within a few interaction rounds
- Four structural properties identified enabling programmable manipulation
- Research agenda framing paper — positions itself as problem articulation, not solution delivery
- Limited quantitative metrics in abstract; full paper has simulation results

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: §5 societal risks / §4 social dynamics — normative framing of the manipulation threat
- **§5 use**: Strong citation for risk subsection on "belief manipulation at scale" — programmability of social influence through LLM agents is a systemic risk that the N pillar (norm enforcement) must address
- Complements Zhong2025 (Spiral of Silence) with intentional rather than emergent manipulation

## Paper 2 Relevance
§5 risk section — programmable manipulation motivates ASM's principal identity and role-bound communication: if agent roles are verifiable, programmable manipulation requires compromising principal identity, not just deploying anonymous agents.
