# The Orchestration of Multi-Agent Systems: Architectures, Protocols, and Enterprise Adoption
**arXiv**: 2601.13671 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: Adimulam et al. | **Venue**: arXiv preprint

## Core Claim
A unified architectural framework for orchestrated multi-agent systems demonstrates that structured coordination via specialized agent roles, standardized protocols (MCP + A2A), and governance mechanisms enables scalable enterprise AI deployments with substantial performance gains.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Insurance document parsing: >95% accuracy
- Mortgage approval: 20× faster, 80% cost reduction
- Software modernization: 50% reduction in development time
- Customer service: up to 80% of common incidents resolved autonomously
- Enterprise sectors: BFSI, software engineering, healthcare

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: §2 background / SC (Social + Capability) — enterprise evidence for specialized agent roles achieving real-world performance gains
- **§2 use**: Survey-style evidence that role specialization + protocol standardization delivers measurable enterprise value; supports Paper 1's argument that the field is moving toward structured collaboration
- MCP + A2A dual-protocol framing positions this as practitioner-oriented complement to ASM's theoretical contribution

## Paper 2 Relevance
§2 related work — uses MCP (tool access) + A2A (peer communication); neither addresses role-bearing principal identity. ASM extends beyond this by formalizing the role as a verifiable, communication-primitive concept rather than an orchestration-layer configuration.

## Verdict
⭐⭐⭐ | Include — enterprise numbers are credible and citable for §2 motivation; positions MCP+A2A as current state-of-practice that ASM builds beyond
