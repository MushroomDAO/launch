# Agents Incorporating Identity and Dynamic Teams in Social Dilemmas
**arXiv**: 2208.03293 | **Year**: 2022 | **Authors**: Kyle Tilbury, Jesse Hoey | **Venue**: arXiv preprint (University of Waterloo)

## Core Claim
Current multi-agent RL systems oversimplify social reality by ignoring agent identity and fixed team structures; incorporating social identity (via identity economics) and dynamic team switching into MARL agents in sequential social dilemmas (Cleanup environment) should yield better population-level outcomes.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- **Preliminary/position paper** — no empirical results reported yet; framework and expected outcomes described
- Cleanup environment (sequential social dilemma) used as testbed
- Two identity specializations: river cleaners vs. apple pickers (innate but unknown to other agents)
- Agents can dynamically form, join, or leave teams (fluid team membership)
- Grounds argument in **identity economics** (Akerlof & Kranton) — identity as utility-relevant social membership

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: Historical precedent / precursor to social role agency — cites identity economics as a basis for agent social structure, directly foreshadowing our social role pillar; use in §2 (Related Work) as early signal that the field was moving toward identity-bearing agents
- **§2 use**: "As early as 2022, Tilbury & Hoey identified the absence of identity and dynamic team membership as fundamental gaps in MARL [cite], anticipating the paradigm shift we formalize here."
- **Pillar context**: Relevant to all three pillars (P: team coordination, SC: identity-based reputation, N: dynamic membership); most directly relevant to SC — identity as a first-class agent property

## Paper 2 Relevance
Marginal. The Tilbury & Hoey framework addresses MARL-level identity in closed environments, not communication-protocol-level role signaling. However, the conceptual move — treating identity as a first-class agent property in social dilemmas — is the intellectual ancestor of ASM's role-bearing message design.

## Verdict
⭐⭐⭐ | Background — cite in §2 historical context as an early pre-cursor to social role agency; limited direct impact (preliminary paper, no results, 2022 vintage).
