# A Survey of Self-Evolving Agents: What, When, How, and Where to Evolve
**arXiv**: 2507.21046 | **Year**: 2025 | **Authors**: Gao, Geng, Hua, Hu, Juan et al. (27 authors) | **Venue**: arXiv preprint

## Core Claim
Static LLMs cannot adapt to novel tasks or dynamic contexts; self-evolving agents can be organized around three orthogonal dimensions — **what to evolve** (model, memory, tools, architecture), **when to evolve** (intra-test-time vs. inter-test-time), and **how to evolve** (reward-based, imitation/demonstration, population-based) — as a unified path toward artificial super-intelligence.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Documents representative systems: Self-Challenging Agent, Self-Rewarding Self-Improving, SAGE/Mem0/Expel (memory), SPO (prompt optimization), ToolGen (tool-as-tokens), ADAS/AFlow (multi-agent workflow)
- Multi-agent workflows outperform hand-designed architectures; test-time adaptation enables just-in-time skill acquisition (LADDER)
- Authors include researchers from Princeton, Tsinghua, Carnegie Mellon, and 20+ additional institutions (27 total authors)
- "The species that survives is the one that is able best to adapt and adjust to the changing environment" — framing self-evolution as biological fitness

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: Old paradigm contrast — the canonical "what/when/how" self-evolution taxonomy that defines capability-centric improvement; companion survey to Fang et al. 2025 (D02) with a more granular orthogonal-dimension lens
