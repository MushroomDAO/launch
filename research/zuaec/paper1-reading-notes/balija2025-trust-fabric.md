# The Trust Fabric: Decentralized Interoperability and Economic Coordination for the Agentic Web
**arXiv**: 2507.07901 | **Year**: 2025 | **Authors**: Balija, Singal, Raskar, Darzi, Bala, Hardjono, Huang | **Venue**: arXiv preprint

## Core Claim
The Nanda Unified Architecture addresses agentic ecosystem fragmentation through a five-layer architecture (discovery, composition, deployment, evaluation, incentivization) that synthesizes DID-based identity, behavioral trust analytics, and X42/H42 micropayments into a unified framework — demonstrated with 99.9% compliance in healthcare.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Healthcare compliance: **99.9% success rate** in regulated environments
- Monthly transaction volume: **$250k+** through commercial marketplaces
- Registry performance: O(log N) resolution time for agent discovery
- Pricing: $0.001–$0.10 per transaction; $49–$199 monthly subscriptions
- Trust Engine: weighted fusion of OPA policy compliance + One-Class SVMs / Hidden Markov Models (behavioral anomaly) + VC attestations
- Learning-to-rank deduplication across federated registries
- MAESTRO security framework + AgentTalk protocol (US patent 12,244,584 B1)

## ASM Comparison (Paper 2 focus)
- **What it does**: Five-layer architecture covering everything from DID discovery to economic incentives — the most architecturally complete framework in this review. Closest in scope to ASM's full vision
- **Gap vs ASM**: (1) Role-bearing identity not present — agents are identified and trusted but not role-typed for routing purposes; (2) Trust scoring is security-behavioral (anomaly detection) not role+performance-reputation; (3) The framework is commercial/proprietary in parts (patented AgentTalk); (4) Permissionless participation unclear — healthcare compliance suggests gated onboarding
- **Paper 2 §2 (related work)**: Primary competitor table entry — describe as "closest architectural predecessor" that validates ASM's design direction while lacking role-bearing routing and permissionless access

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **§4 (Three Pillars)**: The five-layer architecture maps partially onto Paper 1's N/P/S pillars — use as evidence that the field is converging on these components independently
- **§3 (paradigm shift)**: Commercial deployment ($250k/month transactions) proves the market reality of agent economic coordination that Paper 1's paradigm shift addresses

## Verdict
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Key competitor — cite in P2 §2 comparison table and P1 §4; differentiate ASM on role-bearing routing and permissionless participation. The strongest architectural precedent found.
