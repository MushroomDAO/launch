# [Baciak, Cellucci, Falkowski 2026] Punctuated Equilibria in Artificial Intelligence
**arXiv**: 2603.14664 | **Year**: 2026 (submitted March 2026) | **Authors**: Mark Baciak, Thomas A. Cellucci, Deanna M. Falkowski | **Venue**: Preprint
**Cluster**: AI Development Theory & Institutional Fitness
**Status**: Abstract + PDF key sections read (ar5iv fatal error; arxiv.org abs + PDF retrieved)

## Core Claim
AI development follows punctuated equilibrium dynamics — not smooth scaling — with five distinct eras since 1943 interrupted by four rapid phase transitions in the Generative AI era; the authors' "Institutional Scaling Law" proves that institutional fitness (capability × trust × affordability × sovereign compliance) is non-monotonic in model scale, making orchestrated smaller models mathematically superior to frontier generalists in most deployment environments.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- **5 eras** of AI development identified since 1943
- **4 epochs** within the current Generative AI era, each initiated by a discontinuous event
- Key discontinuous events named: transformer architecture, **"DeepSeek Moment"** (likely DeepSeek-R1's emergence as a competitive disruption)
- **4 dimensions** of the Institutional Fitness Manifold: capability, institutional trust, affordability, sovereign compliance
- Core mathematical result: **Institutional Scaling Law** — fitness is non-monotonic in scale; beyond an environment-specific optimum, further scaling *degrades* fitness due to trust erosion and cost penalties
- **Implication**: "orchestrated systems of smaller, domain-adapted models can mathematically outperform frontier generalists in most institutional deployment environments"
- AI systems referenced: Claude (Anthropic), GPT series (OpenAI), DeepSeek, BERT, Gemini, PaLM, AlphaFold, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion
- Note: paper does NOT discuss "niche constructors" — this framing was an incorrect attribution; the paper's contribution is the institutional fitness framework

## Paper 1 Relevance
**§1 (Motivation — paradigm shift framing)**: The punctuated equilibrium framework provides theoretical grounding for Paper 1's core claim that we are at a *discontinuous* transition — not incremental improvement — in the agent paradigm. The shift from "capability invocation" to "social role agency" maps directly to a punctuated transition moment. The five eras + four epochs structure can be used to locate the current transition.

**§4 (SC Pillar — institutional trust)**: The Institutional Scaling Law is directly relevant: trust (SC) is one of the four fitness dimensions, and the law shows trust is *not* derivable from capability alone. This is mathematical support for why Social Capital must be quantified separately rather than assumed from benchmark performance.

**§1 alternative use**: The paper's finding that "orchestrated systems of smaller models outperform frontier generalists" in institutional settings supports the Three Pillars' network/specialization model over single-model architectures.

## Paper 2 Relevance
The "sovereign compliance" dimension of institutional fitness maps to ASM's design goal of avoiding regulatory sensitivity (e.g., the "去中心化/decentralized" removal). ASM as an "Open" protocol is partly a sovereign compliance design choice.
