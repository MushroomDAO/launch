# Stop Drawing Scientific Claims from LLM Social Simulations Without Robustness Audits
**arXiv**: 2605.18890 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: Ye et al. | **Venue**: arXiv preprint (physics.soc-ph, cs.AI, cs.CY, cs.MA)

## Core Claim
Scientific conclusions drawn from LLM-based social simulations risk reflecting implementation artifacts rather than actual social mechanisms; minor parameter modifications can shift outcomes by up to 76 percentage points, and sensitivity varies dramatically across model families.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Persona format / instruction framing changes shifted cooperation rates by up to 76 percentage points in Prisoner's Dilemma simulations
- Same perturbation: 76pp swing in one frontier model vs. only 1pp in another (cross-model inconsistency)
- Network topology choices significantly influenced polarization measurements in echo chamber simulations
- TRAILS taxonomy developed: agent-level (micro), interaction-level (meso), system-level (macro) robustness dimensions
- Two case studies: repeated Prisoner's Dilemma + social media echo chamber
