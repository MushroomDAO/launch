# ScioMind: Cognitively Grounded Multi-Agent Social Simulation with Anchoring-Based Belief Dynamics and Dynamic Profiles
**arXiv**: 2605.13725 | **Year**: 2026 | **Authors**: Yang et al. | **Venue**: arXiv preprint (cs.AI)

## Core Claim
ScioMind bridges traditional opinion dynamics models and unconstrained LLM reasoning by integrating cognitive psychology principles (anchoring-based belief updates, hierarchical memory, dynamic corpus-grounded profiles) into multi-agent social simulations.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
- Dynamic profiles increased opinion diversity vs. static persona baselines
- Memory + reflection reduced unstable opinion oscillation
- Anchoring induced persistent belief trajectories aligning with political psychology patterns
- Evaluated across polarization, diversity, extremization, and trajectory stability metrics
- Policy debate scenario case studies (specific numbers not available in abstract)

## Paper 1 Relevance
- **Role**: §4 social dynamics context — cognitive grounding of belief update as complement to social role persistence
- **§4 use**: Supports the argument that principled agent cognition (not raw LLM prompting) is needed for stable social behavior; connects to N (Norm) pillar — anchoring ≈ internalized norm
- Gap from ASM perspective: no identity or role-bearing mechanism, purely cognitive modeling
