# [AI Agent Index 2025 — Taxonomy] The 2025 AI Agent Index (Taxonomy + Safety Survey)
**arXiv**: 2502.01635 | **Year**: 2025 | **Authors**: Multi-author team | **Venue**: Preprint (companion to 2602.17753)
**Cluster**: Industry Ecosystem Baseline & Taxonomy
**Status**: Abstract + key stats read (ar5iv succeeded)

## Core Claim
The first comprehensive public database of 67 deployed agentic AI systems reveals that developers are transparent about capabilities but provide "strikingly limited information" about safety practices — with only 9% reporting external safety evaluations.

## Key Evidence / Numbers
**Scope:**
- **67** agentic AI systems documented (broader than the 30-agent 2602.17753 index — earlier/companion version)
- Developer feedback response rate: **36%** (August 2024–January 2025 collection period)

**Geographic Distribution:**
- USA: **45 systems (67.2%)** — dominant concentration
- Remaining **22 systems** distributed across other countries

**Developer Type:**
- Industry: **49 systems (73.1%)**
- Academia: **18 systems (26.9%)**

**Application Domains:**
- Software engineering/computer use: **50 systems (74.6%)** — software dev is plurality use case
- Research: subset of remaining
- Robotics, universal, other: smaller categories

**Transparency & Safety:**
- Public documentation available: **47 systems (70.1%)**
- Released code: **33 systems (49.3%)** — roughly half open-source
- Formal safety policies disclosed: **13 systems (19.4%)**
- External safety evaluations reported: **6 systems (9%)**
- Internal safety evaluations: **5 systems (7.5%)** — only 7.5% even self-report
- Academic systems releasing code: **16 of 18 (88.8%)** vs. industry far lower

**Deployment Timeline:**
- Approximately **50%** of systems deployed in second half of 2024 alone — explosive growth pace
- Earliest deployments: early 2023

## Paper 1 Relevance
**§1 (Motivation — ecosystem scale and safety gap)**: The 50% deployed in H2 2024 statistic is a powerful punctuation point showing the pace of adoption. The 74.6% software engineering concentration explains why SWE-Bench is the natural benchmark domain. The 9% external safety evaluation rate reinforces the case that current deployment is outpacing governance — motivation for the Three Pillars (especially SC/quantified social capital as a trust substitute).

**§1 (ecosystem baseline)**: Use alongside 2602.17753 — the two indices together (67 systems in taxonomy, 30 deep-annotated) give a robust picture. 67 systems as of early 2025 is the denominator for scale claims.

**§5 (Deployment evidence)**: The 49.3% open-source rate is relevant to the N pillar (permissionless network) — roughly half the ecosystem is already open, providing the foundation for ASM's open-source community model.

## Paper 2 Relevance
The 49.3% open-source rate supports ASM's Linux/Nostr positioning — the ecosystem is not monolithically closed; there is a large open stratum that ASM can serve as shared infrastructure.

## Verdict
⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Important companion index providing broader ecosystem baseline (67 systems vs. 30); the "50% deployed in H2 2024" and "9% external safety evaluation" statistics are strong §1 anchors.
